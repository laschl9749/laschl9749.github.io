Geography

Today Austria is a small, predominantly mountainous country found in the Central Europe Reigon. It has three main geographic areas it covers: The Alps, mainly found in the southern or west, the largest mountain range in Europe and the most extensive. The Danube, found in the east, it´s name is derived from river flowing through the area. The river itself is the second larger in Europe and flows into the balkans and black sea with surronding plains and fields and finally to the north The Bohemian Forest, another mountain range covering a large chunk of it´s namesake, Bohemia

Politics

Austria is actually a Federal, representative democratic republic, similar to the U.S.A as it derives the right to rule or be the goverment of Austria through the Austrian Federal Constitution, the difference is that they have parliment compared to the congress. In addition they are democratic unlike the US which is simply a republic. Parliment consists of the Nationalrat being the lower house and the upper house, the Bundesrat. The Nationalrat brings laws to a vote and if it succeds in the lower house is passed onto the Bundesrat where it can either be vetoed and overturned by a 2/3 majority from the lower house or pass and be made into law.
